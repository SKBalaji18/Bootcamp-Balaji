Typography

What is Typography?

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing when displayed. The choice of fonts, spacing, and layout plays a vital role in how content is perceived by readers.

Why is Typography Important?

Typography helps establish a visual hierarchy, guiding the reader through content in a structured and logical manner. It improves readability and ensures that your message is effectively conveyed.

For example, using a bold and striking font for headers helps them stand out, while a clean and simple font for body text ensures easy reading. Proper spacing, such as line height and margins, also enhances the overall user experience.

Typography in Web Design

In web design, typography is more than just choosing fonts. It involves managing:

  • Font sizes and weights
  • Line height and letter spacing
  • Text alignment and color contrast

By combining fonts like Montserrat for headers and Roboto for body text, you can create a professional and visually appealing design.

Key Takeaways

When designing with typography:

  1. Use font pairings that complement each other.
  2. Maintain consistent spacing and alignment.
  3. Ensure adequate contrast between text and background.
